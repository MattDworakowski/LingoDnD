# LingoDnD — Story (English reading version)

> **For review only.** The game is played in German (this is the substance of the
> learning). This file is a faithful English rendering of the narration in
> `content/episode-01/` and `content/episode-02/`, so you can judge the story,
> pacing, and tone without reading German. It is **not** used by the app or the
> render pipeline — edit the German `episode.json` files to change the real game.
>
> **How to read it:**
> - Scenes play top to bottom. The hero is always "you" (second person).
> - **◆ Choice / roll** points branch the story, but **both outcomes continue** —
>   there are no dead ends and no "game over" (a failed roll just means a helper
>   appears instead).
> - **▸ Class variant** = the narration changes depending on the player's class
>   (Warrior / Mage / Bard); all variants rejoin the same next scene.
> - **? Mini-game** = a comprehension question; the ✓ marks the correct answer
>   (right answer = +1 XP, but the story continues either way).
> - **⚔ Combat** = a dice duel; the hero can't truly lose (rescued at 0 HP).

# Episode 1 — "Der gestohlene Sternstein" / *The Stolen Starstone*

**Premise:** A thief steals the Starstone that keeps eternal winter at bay; the
young hero sets out through the forest to track him to the mountains.

### Scene 1 — The theft *(map node)*
High in the mountains lies the realm of Aldoria. In the great hall, a stone
glows: the Starstone. Its warm light keeps the long winter away. For a hundred
years the Starstone has protected the whole land.

Tonight, you are keeping watch in the hall. You are still young, but you are
brave. Suddenly, a window opens quietly. A dark figure creeps in. It wears a
cloak, green as fir trees. On its shoulder sits a black raven. Quickly, the
figure snatches the Starstone. The light goes out. Everything turns cold and
dark. The thief has vanished.

### Scene 2 — The council
In the morning, the wise folk of Aldoria gather. There is Elara, the elf, with
silver hair. And there is Borin, the dwarf, with a thick red beard. The two do
not like each other much. Elara cries: "It was surely a dwarf!" Borin grumbles:
"Never! That was certainly an elf!"

Then the old wizard raises his hand. Everyone falls silent. He says calmly: "Do
not quarrel. We need a hero to bring back the Starstone." The wizard looks at
you: "You saw the thief. Will you go?" You nod. The wizard gives you a small
pouch. "Here is some bread and three gold coins. Take good care of yourself."

> **? Mini-game (memory):** "What colour was the thief's cloak?"
> — ✓ Green · Red · Blue

### Scene 3 — The forest's edge *(map node)*
You leave the hall. Before you lies a great, dark forest. The thief came this
way. But which path did he take? You look around very carefully. Will you find a
trail?

> **◆ Roll — Perception:** "Do you find the trail?"

> **▸ If you succeed (10–20):**
> You look very closely. In the soft ground you see footprints. And there — a
> black feather from the raven! The trail leads deep into the forest. You follow it.

> **▸ If you fail (1–9):**
> You search and search, but you find no trail. Then a little forest bird lands
> on a branch. The bird chirps: "Looking for the thief? I saw him! Come, I'll
> show you the way." You follow the little bird into the forest.

### Scene 4 — The broken bridge *(map node)*
Soon you come to a rushing river. An old bridge crosses it, but it is broken. On
the other side, the trail continues.

> **▸ Warrior:** You are strong. You grab a heavy tree trunk. With all your might
> you lay it across the river. And so you build yourself a new bridge and cross safely.
>
> **▸ Mage:** You can do magic. You speak a spell, and ice grows across the water.
> It becomes a glittering bridge. Carefully you walk across the cold ice.
>
> **▸ Bard:** An old ferryman sits on the bank. You smile and tell him about your
> important quest. The ferryman nods and takes you across in his boat.

### Scene 5 — The dark woods *(map node)*
The path leads into a dark part of the forest. The trees here are old and dense.
It is utterly quiet. Suddenly you see two glowing eyes among the bushes. Do you
dare go on?

> **◆ Roll — Courage:** "Do you go on?"

> **▸ If you succeed (10–20):**
> You take a deep breath and bravely walk on. Out of the bushes comes an old owl.
> The owl calls out kindly: "Hoo-hoo!" The glowing eyes were only the owl. With
> its wing, it points you the way.

> **▸ If you fail (1–9):**
> You get a fright and stop. But then you think of the Starstone. Slowly you walk
> on. A little owl flutters up and calls: "Hoo-hoo!" It was only an owl! You can't
> help but laugh, and you walk on.

### Scene 6 — The goblin *(map node)*
The path narrows between tall rocks. Suddenly a goblin leaps onto the path! He is
small and green and has crooked teeth. The goblin screeches: "Nobody gets past
here!" He swings his club.

> **⚔ Combat — Goblin** (HP 9, hits on 9+, deals 2 damage; you deal 3 per hit).
> Mage can use Fireball; **Bard can talk it out** (Charisma roll vs 12).

> **▸ If you talk your way past (Bard):**
> You tell the goblin about your important quest. You promise him a gold coin. The
> goblin grins and says: "All right, all right!" He lets you pass. As he does, a
> small silver key falls from his pocket. You pick it up and tuck it away.

> **▸ If you win the fight:**
> The goblin gives up. "Fine, fine!" he cries, and runs off between the rocks. As
> he flees he drops something shiny: a small silver key. You pick it up and tuck
> it away. The path is clear.

> **★ Item gained: Silver Key** — *(returns in Episode 2)*

### Scene 7 — Campfire *(map node)*
Evening falls in the forest. You make a small fire and sit down on a stone. The
forest is dark, but the fire is warm and bright. A little forest bird settles
beside you. The bird chirps: "Don't be afraid. You are brave. You can do this."
You eat a piece of bread and share it with the bird. For a moment, you no longer
feel alone. Tomorrow your journey continues.

> **? Mini-game (comprehension):** "What do you share with the bird?"
> — ✓ Bread · Gold · Water

### Scene 8 — The thief, far ahead *(map node)*
The next day you reach the edge of the forest. Before you rise high, dark
mountains. Far up, on a narrow path, you see a figure in a green cloak. The
thief! On his shoulder sits the black raven. The thief glances back and
disappears behind the rocks. The Starstone is so close. But the way into the
mountains is long and dangerous. You take a deep breath and ready yourself. Your
adventure has only just begun.

> **? Mini-game (comprehension):** "Where did the thief flee?"
> — ✓ Into the mountains · Across the river · Into the town

### Ending
You have mastered this adventure well. But the Starstone is still not found. What
happens next? You'll find out in the next episode.

# Episode 2 — "Der Pfad ins Gebirge" / *The Path into the Mountains*

**Premise:** The hero climbs the snowy mountains after the thief, to the old
mountain gate and the thief's ice-palace lair — and glimpses why the Starstone
was taken.

### Scene 1 — The mountain's foot *(map node)*
The morning is cold and clear. Before you rise the high mountains. Deep snow lies
everywhere. The wind is icy and bites at your cheeks. Here the forest ends, and
here the mountain begins. You think of the Starstone. Without its warm light, the
winter grows ever longer and colder. You must bring it back. In the white snow
you see fresh footprints. The thief climbed up here. You pull your cloak tighter
and follow the trail up the mountain. You are brave. You can do this.

### Scene 2 — The snow fox
A little fox sits in the snow. Its fur is white as the snow, only its nose is
black. The snow fox looks at you with clever eyes. It says: "Are you looking for
the thief in the green cloak? I saw him! He climbed high up, to the old mountain
gate. Take care," says the fox, "the mountain is cold and the path is steep."
Then it sniffs the air. "Come, I'll show you the start of the way." Together you
trudge through the deep snow.

> **? Mini-game (memory):** "Where did the thief climb to?"
> — ✓ To the old mountain gate · To the river · Into the town

### Scene 3 — The trail in the snow *(map node)*
The snow fox has to return to its den. Alone, you climb on up the mountain. Soon
the path stops altogether. All around you is nothing but white snow. Fresh snow
falls and slowly covers everything. Which way did the thief go? You look around
very carefully. Will you find the trail in the snow?

> **◆ Roll — Perception:** "Do you find the trail in the snow?"

> **▸ If you succeed (10–20):**
> You look very closely. In the soft snow you see deep footprints. On a jagged
> rock hangs a thread, green as fir trees. It's from the thief's cloak! The trail
> leads steeply upward. You follow it up the mountain.

> **▸ If you fail (1–9):**
> You search and search, but the snow has already covered everything. Then you
> hear a bright bark. The little snow fox comes back! "I know this mountain well,"
> it says. "Follow me!" The fox springs lightly over the snow and shows you the
> way up. You're glad not to be alone.

### Scene 4 — The ravine *(map node)*
A deep ravine opens before you. An old rope bridge crosses it, but the planks are
broken. On the other side, the trail continues.

> **▸ Warrior:** You are strong. You grab the thick rope and pull yourself across
> hand over hand. The wind tugs at you, but you hold on tight and arrive safely on
> the other side.
>
> **▸ Mage:** You can do magic. You speak a spell, and out of the snow grows a
> glittering bridge of ice. Carefully you cross the smooth ice.
>
> **▸ Bard:** At the edge stands a big mountain goat with curved horns. You speak
> to it calmly and kindly. The goat understands you. You climb onto its back, and
> with one great leap it carries you safely across the ravine.

### Scene 5 — The ice cave *(map node)*
The path leads into a dark ice cave. Long, sharp icicles hang from the ceiling.
Deep inside, something blue glitters. It is cold and utterly still. Suddenly you
hear a deep rumbling from the darkness. Do you dare go in?

> **◆ Roll — Courage:** "Do you go into the cave?"

> **▸ If you succeed (10–20):**
> You take a deep breath and bravely enter the cave. The deep rumbling comes from
> a great polar bear. He lies curled up, fast asleep. Quietly you tiptoe past his
> warm fur. On the other side you can already see light: the way to the old
> mountain gate.

> **▸ If you fail (1–9):**
> You get a fright and stop. But then you think of the Starstone. Very slowly you
> walk on. The rumbling comes from a great polar bear, fast asleep. You almost
> have to laugh. Quietly and carefully you tiptoe past him to the exit.

### Scene 6 — The gate and the snow troll *(map node)*
At the end of the cave stands an old gate of stone and ice. In front of it sits a
big snow troll. His fur is white and shaggy, and he has a thick, bulbous nose.
When he sees you, he jumps up and growls: "Nobody comes through my gate!" He
stamps his feet so hard that the snow flies.

> **⚔ Combat — Snow Troll** (HP 10, hits on 10+, deals 2 damage; you deal 3 per
> hit). Mage can use Fireball; **Bard can talk it out** (Charisma roll vs 12).

> **▸ If you talk your way past (Bard):**
> You speak very calmly with the troll. You tell him about the Starstone and about
> the cold winter that will not end. The troll scratches his head. "All right," he
> growls. "You are brave and kind." He stands up and clears the way. The gate is
> shut with a silver lock. You remember the silver key from the forest. It fits
> perfectly! In the snow, a blue shard glitters. You pick it up — it is ice cold.
> Then you walk through the open gate.

> **▸ If you win the fight:**
> The snow troll sits down heavily in the snow. "Fine, fine," he growls. "You are
> strong enough." Wearily he trudges away. The gate is shut with a silver lock.
> Then you remember the silver key from the forest. It fits perfectly! With a soft
> click the gate springs open. In the snow glitters a blue shard of ice and magic.
> You pick it up and walk through the gate.

> **★ Item gained: Frost Shard** — *a clue: where does this deep cold come from?*

### Scene 7 — Shelter and campfire *(map node)*
Behind the gate you find a small cave, sheltered from the wind. Outside the snow
is storming, but in here it is calm. You make a small fire. Then the snow fox
darts in and lies down beside you by the warm fire. "Don't be afraid," it says
softly. "You've come so far already. You really are brave." You warm your hands
by the fire and share your last piece of bread with the fox. For a moment, your
heart feels warm. Tomorrow you'll find the thief.

> **? Mini-game (comprehension):** "What do you share with the snow fox?"
> — ✓ Bread · Gold · Ice

### Scene 8 — The ice hall *(map node)*
In the morning you climb the last, steep path. At the very top, a great hall of
blue ice opens up. In the middle, on a throne of ice, the Starstone glows
faintly. In front of it stands the thief in the green cloak. On his shoulder sits
the black raven. The raven caws loudly, and it grows colder still. The thief
hears you and turns around. Quickly he grabs the Starstone and runs through a
dark gate, deep into the mountain. You race after him, but the heavy ice gate
slams shut before you. So close! But you haven't saved the Starstone yet.

> **? Mini-game (comprehension):** "Where does the thief run with the Starstone?"
> — ✓ Deep into the mountain · Across the river · Into the forest

### Ending
You have bravely mastered this adventure too. The thief has escaped, but now you
are very close behind him. Deep in the mountain, the next adventure already
awaits. Soon you will save the Starstone. To be continued.
